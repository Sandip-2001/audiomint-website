🧩 Browser Extension Setup

The AudioMint Companion extension acts as the bridge between your browser and the desktop app.

  • Pin the Extension: Click the puzzle piece icon in your browser and "Pin" AudioMint to your toolbar for easy access.
  • Initial Activation: Click the AudioMint icon to wake up the extension. This explicitly grants it permission to read your current active tab.
  • Hardware Permissions: The very first time you use AudioMint, the browser may display a popup asking for Microphone permissions. You must click Allow. We do not record your microphone; this permission is required by the browser simply to read the names of your connected speakers and headphones.
Why isn't my YouTube tab showing up?
If a tab was already open and playing media before you installed or activated the extension or opening the app, it missed the initial handshake. Simply refresh the browser tab to connect it instantly.

🪟 Windows Installation & Setup

Windows is highly suspicious of indie applications that route hardware audio. You may encounter these standard security blocks during installation:

1. Windows Defender "SmartScreen"

  • When you run the .exe installer, Windows may show a blue screen saying "Windows protected your PC."
  • Click More Info.
  • Click Run Anyway to proceed with the installation.

2. Empty Folders / Antivirus Blocks

If the app says "Missing Shortcut" or the installation folder is empty, your third-party antivirus (McAfee, Norton, Avast, etc.) silently deleted the app during extraction.

  • Open your Antivirus software.
  • Go to the Virus Vault or Protection History.
  • Find AudioMint and select Restore / Allow on Device.
  • Tip: Running the installer as Administrator can also help bypass silent extraction blocks.

🍏 macOS Installation & Setup

Because AudioMint deeply routes system audio, macOS Gatekeeper may flag the initial download from indie developers. Here is how to bypass it safely:

  • Download the .dmg file and open it.
  • Drag the AudioMint app directly into your Applications folder.
  • If macOS says the app "cannot be opened," open your Terminal (press Cmd + Space and type "Terminal").
  • Copy and paste the following command and press Enter:
codesign --force --deep --sign - /Applications/AudioMint.app
  • You can now safely launch AudioMint from your Launchpad or Applications folder!
Developer Note: This securely signs the application locally on your machine to bypass the Apple Developer paywall. It is a standard practice for open-source and indie software.

🛠️ No Sound? (Windows Troubleshooting)

If the desktop app is connected and the route is drawn, but you don't hear any sound out of your target speaker, check these two hidden Windows settings:

1. The Windows Volume Mixer Mute

  • Windows often mutes newly installed, unsigned applications by default.
  • Right-click the Speaker Icon in your Windows taskbar (bottom right).
  • Select Open Volume mixer.
  • Scroll down to the Apps section, find AudioMint, and ensure the volume slider is up and it is not muted.

2. Windows Privacy Restrictions

  • Windows may be blocking the app's access to the audio hardware engine.
  • Open the Windows Settings app.
  • Navigate to Privacy & security > Microphone.
  • Scroll to the very bottom of the page to the section titled "Let desktop apps access your microphone".
  • Ensure this toggle is set to ON. Restart AudioMint.
